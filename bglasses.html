BGlasses

AI-Powered Assistive Glasses for the Visually Impaired

Demo Video | VnExpress Coverage | VnEconomy Coverage

Overview

Problem

Visually impaired individuals face significant challenges in reading printed materials and navigating their environment independently, limiting their access to information and autonomy.

Solution

BGlasses combines computer vision, OCR, and text-to-speech AI models into wearable smart glasses that can read text aloud, recognize objects, and assist with daily navigation tasks in real-time.

Impact

Empowered visually impaired users with independent reading capabilities, gained national press coverage in Vietnam, and showcased at TechFest Connect 2021 as an innovative assistive technology solution.

My Role & Contributions

  • Led hardware development and system integration from initial prototype to production-ready device
  • Designed custom PCB modules using Eagle CAD for power management and audio output
  • Built and tested multiple iterations of embedded systems with Raspberry Pi Zero W and custom MCUs
  • Integrated 4G connectivity module (Simcom/Quectel) for standalone operation without phone dependency
  • Developed power optimization solutions to enable extended battery life in wearable form factor
  • Created 3D models and prototypes that attach seamlessly to standard eyeglasses
  • Tested and optimized AI models for text recognition, object detection, and scene understanding
  • Co-founded BTEK team and managed hardware-software collaboration throughout development

Project Gallery

Development Journey

April 2020
Project Inception: The original idea conceived by Trinh Quoc Huy. First conceptual version developed.
June 2020
Hardware Development Begins: I joined the project and started building the hardware components and system architecture.
December 2020
PCB Design & AI Testing: Designed custom power board PCB using Eagle CAD with integrated sound module. Named team BTEK. PCB manufactured in China. Conducted comprehensive AI model testing for all features. Created new wearable version that attaches to standard glasses.
January 2021
PCB Delivery & Demo: Custom PCB boards delivered. First working demonstration video released.
March 2021
New Version Release: Launched improved version with enhanced features and better integration.
April 2021
Next-Gen Planning: Planned major upgrade to address previous limitations:
  • Previous: Pi Zero W MCU had power optimization challenges and required separate battery box
  • New: Custom high-speed MCU (outsourced), integrated 4G SIM module, advanced power management
First prototype developed with Simcom 7500E module.
July 2021
Power Management Breakthrough: Developed new power management module prototype with improved efficiency and battery life.
December 2021
Chip Upgrade Testing: Tested Quectel chip as alternative to Simcom for improved performance and reliability.
December 2022
Temporary Pause: Project temporarily delayed due to challenges in hardware development and resource constraints.
