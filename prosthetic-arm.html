Prosthetic Arm for Disabled People

Low-Cost Robotic Prosthetic Arm Controlled by Foot Movements

Demo Video | IEEE Publication | ISEF Abstract | Ministry of Education

Overview

Problem

In Vietnam, many people with bilateral arm disabilities face limited access to affordable prosthetic solutions. Existing prosthetic arms are expensive (often thousands of dollars) and designed primarily for single-arm amputees, leaving those without both arms with few viable options.

Solution

A low-cost robotic prosthetic arm controlled by foot movements using sensors attached to the user's legs. Capable of 31 distinct motions including grasping, rotating, and extending, enabling basic daily tasks like eating and drinking at a production cost of only $150.

Impact

Successfully demonstrated with real users, won 3rd place at INTEL ISEF 2017 and 1st place at ViSEF 2017. Published in IEEE proceedings and featured by Vietnam's Ministry of Education, providing an accessible prosthetic solution for bilateral arm amputees.

My Role & Contributions

  • Conceived and developed the complete system from initial concept to working prototype over 2 years (2016-2018)
  • Designed innovative control system using leg-mounted sensors (tilting sensors on foot and ankle) to enable 31 distinct arm movements
  • Created all 3D models in Sketchup and coordinated 3D printing of prosthetic components
  • Built custom 3D printer from scratch to enable rapid prototyping and iteration
  • Programmed Arduino microcontroller in C to process sensor inputs and control servo motors
  • Conducted real-world testing with disabled users to validate functionality and usability
  • Upgraded system in 2018 with EMG (electromyography) sensors for muscle-based control and human-like skin
  • Presented research at international competitions (INTEL ISEF, ViSEF) and published findings in IEEE proceedings

Development Journey

June 2016
Initial Prototype: Created first handmade version using mica plastic to validate the core concept and control mechanism.
August 2016
First 3D Design: Transitioned from handmade prototype to digital 3D model in Sketchup, enabling precise manufacturing and replication.
September 2016
First Operational Version: Successfully assembled and tested first working prototype with motor control and basic movements.
October 2016
Testing Phase: Extensive testing of movement patterns, sensor calibration, and control responsiveness.
December 2016
Production & Assembly: Finalized 3D design and sent to 3D printing service. Assembled all printed parts with electronic components and tested new transmitter design for improved sensor communication.
January 2017
Real-World User Testing: Conducted first tests with actual disabled person, validating that the prosthetic could perform daily tasks (eating, drinking, object manipulation). Built custom 3D printer to accelerate iteration cycles.
March 2017
ViSEF 2017 Victory: Won 1st place at Vietnam Science & Engineering Fair in Robotics and Intelligent Machines category, advancing to final round and qualifying for international competition.
May 2017
INTEL ISEF 2017 International Recognition: Competed at INTEL International Science & Engineering Fair in Los Angeles, winning two prestigious awards: 3rd place in competition and 3rd place IEEE Presidents' Scholarship. Presented research to international judges and scientific community.
November 2018
Next-Generation Development: Moved to new lab and began building advanced prototype with major upgrades: new mechanical design, EMG (electromyography) sensors for muscle-based control instead of foot sensors, and human-like silicone skin for improved aesthetics. Tested new signal processing algorithms.
November 2018
Public Showcases: Demonstrated upgraded prosthetic arm at TechDay 2018 and TechFest 2018, receiving significant public and industry interest.
December 2018
Actuator Development: Created replica version of PQ12 linear actuator with longer stroke, enabling more natural and extended arm movements.
